The Basic 12 Principles of Animation

- Creates natural motion, feeling, weight, and emotional expression

Squash and Stretch

Shows Weight and volume

Anticipation

Prepares the audience for a major action

Staging

Directs the audiences eye

Straight Ahead and Pose-to-Pose

Follow Through and Overlap

Slow In and Slow Out

Accelerate and decelerate motion coming in and out.

Arcs

Secondary Action

Smaller supporting movement to main action.

Timing

Controls speed and mood.

Exaggeration

Emphasises facial actions of physical actions.

Solid Drawing
